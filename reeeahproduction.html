Production of Reeeah!

If you have read from my "History" page, then you may already know that I make all of this music by myself. I have several guitar tracks overlapping, all of which are played by me. Though I do not play the drums, I am the composer. The drums are all samples from various drum kits. I equalized and compressed the samples to get a specific sound. I also choose when each sound will come in. I make up for the fact that I do not have a bass guitar by playing several guitars at once, at least one of which is usually played low, and throwing in various electronic sounds within my music. This is how my music becomes a cross between metalcore and industrial metal. I play metalcore because it is my genre of choice, but I need to throw in those electronic sounds in order to make up for my lack of a bassist.

All of my production is done on FL Studio and Audacity. Many musicians prefer not to use FL Studio because it is primarily supposed to be used for rap, hip hop, and techno. However, after getting very familiar with FL Studio, I have been able to use it to make good quality metal. As for vocals, I used to put them into my track but I had to take them out because my voice is terrible. The primary FL Studio plugin which I use for making metal is the "Fruity Slicer" in order to help stabalize the tempo and sample different sounds. Before importing each guitar part into FL Studio, I record with audacity. I plug my guitar into an effects pedal which is attached to my computer with various effects on it. I prefer to use audacity because it's very simple to use for distorting on top of the distortion that I use in my effects pedal. With this process I am able to produce near proffessional-sounding tracks with low priced equipment.
